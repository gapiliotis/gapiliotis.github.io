Hubris

The importance of not only our coast, but the system in which our ocean ecosystem affects our lives, plays both a catastrophic and lifegiving role on our planet. Technology has allowed us to obtain a deeper understanding of our world by giving us new ways to experience it like through scuba diving and virtual reality. By obtaining a scuba diving certification through San Jose State University, I have been able to truly enjoy a greater depth of the world that has only been experienced by less than one percent of the human population. I have seen the effects of destroyed kelp forests first-hand, as well as the beauty of our oceans. Through VR, Hubris aims to give viewers an immersive experience that educates and questions their own knowledge and impact on the environment. As a technical artist, combining art and science is instrumental in protecting our planet as science is only as valuable as it is communicable. This work draws a comparison between past ruins and the effects of modern ruins. Soon our current societies will collapse and join the collection of ancient civilizations that the ocean has amassed.

My research delves into aspects of a school of thought known as cosmicism, a philosophy created by H.P. Lovecraft and is present in his literary works. Cosmicism is similar to nihilism in many ways, in its emphasis on the emptiness of everything, but with a focus on the insignificance of humanity rather than rejecting a higher purpose. There are aspects of cosmicism that apply more to my work than the broad definition, like fear of the unknown and the search for knowledge ending in disaster. I am exploring aspects of this fear and how our advancements as a society are leading to the collapse of our ecosystem. Hubris was inspired by my time 40 feet under the ocean surface and my newfound appreciation for the horror genre. Its ability to have us reflect on ourselves on what frightens us is instrumental in understanding the state of where we are as a species. Cosmicism makes us aware of the dangers of knowledge, especially when it is used carelessly. It is the ignorance of people that results in fear and misinformation, leading to the extreme catastrophizing of events. This attitude splits us apart and slows us down from progressing towards a healthier planet and species.
